Mailchimp: The Most Popular Alternative

Mailchimp is the name most people think of first, and its free tier makes it genuinely attractive for brand-new businesses with fewer than 500 contacts. The drag-and-drop editor is solid, and the analytics dashboard shows open rates, click rates, and revenue attribution cleanly.

Where Mailchimp falls short: automations on the free plan are extremely limited. You get a single welcome email, and that is it. To access multi-step automations — a basic drip sequence for new subscribers, for instance — you need to upgrade to the Essentials plan at $13 per month. At that price point, Constant Contact at $12 per month includes more automation triggers, better list segmentation, and superior customer support. Mailchimp's chat support has become notoriously slow since they reduced their team in 2023. Constant Contact offers phone support on all paid plans, which matters when something breaks before an important send.

Brevo: Best if You Pay Per Email, Not Per Contact

Brevo (formerly Sendinblue) structures its pricing around emails sent, not contacts stored. That is genuinely useful if you have a large contact list but email them infrequently — say, a seasonal business that mails 5,000 contacts four times a year. On their free plan, you can store unlimited contacts and send up to 300 emails per day, which is 9,000 per month.

The catch: Brevo's template library is thin compared to Constant Contact's 300+ options. The interface also has a steeper learning curve, and their deliverability rates — while acceptable — sit about 3 to 5 percentage points below Constant Contact in third-party benchmark tests. If getting emails into the inbox is your top priority, that gap matters.

Klaviyo: Best for Online Stores, Overkill for Everyone Else

Klaviyo earns its strong reputation in the e-commerce world. It integrates with Shopify, WooCommerce, BigCommerce, and Magento at a depth that no general-purpose email tool matches. It tracks exactly which email triggered which purchase, down to the cent. If you run an online store doing serious volume, those revenue attribution numbers are invaluable.

For a local service business, a restaurant, a consultant, or a nonprofit, Klaviyo is the wrong tool. It is complex, assumes you have a product catalog, and prices jump steeply as your list grows. At 5,000 contacts, Klaviyo costs $100 per month. Constant Contact at the same list size costs around $55 per month — and covers everything a non-e-commerce business actually needs.

AWeber: The Veteran That Has Stayed Relevant

AWeber has been around since 1998, and it shows — in a mostly good way. The platform is rock solid, the deliverability is consistently strong, and the template library is genuinely the largest of any tool on this list at 700+ options. AWeber also includes a free landing page builder and a basic web push notification tool at no extra cost.

The downside is that AWeber has not kept pace with modern automation features. Compared to Constant Contact's visual automation builder, AWeber's workflow setup feels dated. If you are a solo blogger or content creator who wants a reliable, no-surprises email tool and values template variety above all else, AWeber is worth a look. For most small businesses managing customer relationships across multiple touchpoints, Constant Contact handles the job more cleanly.

How to Evaluate Any Email Tool Before You Commit

Whether you land on Constant Contact or decide one of the alternatives is the right fit, use this checklist before you commit to any platform:

1. Send a Test Campaign Before You Migrate Your List

Every platform listed here offers a free trial. Use it. Build one real email, import 50 real contacts, and send it. Check how it looks in Gmail, Outlook, and Apple Mail. Use a free tool like Mail-Tester.com to check your spam score before you go live. This ten-minute test will tell you more than any comparison article.

2. Check Deliverability with a Small Batch First

When you first sign up for any email platform, your sender reputation starts at zero. Send to your most engaged contacts first — the people who open every email you send. This warms up your new sending domain and protects you from early spam flags that could damage your list long-term.

3. Map Your Automations Before You Build Them

Sketch your automation sequences on paper before you touch the platform interface. Decide: what triggers the sequence? What is the delay between emails? What happens if someone does not open? Having this mapped out saves hours of rebuilding when you realize mid-setup that the logic does not work.

4. Calculate True Cost at Your Expected List Size in 12 Months

Most small businesses grow their email list 20% to 40% per year. Look at the pricing tier above your current contact count and factor that into your monthly cost. A tool that costs $9 per month today might cost $45 per month by next December. Constant Contact's pricing scales predictably, and their annual plan discounts make that growth cheaper to manage.

5. Test Customer Support Before You Need It

Send a support message — or make a phone call — during your free trial on a Tuesday afternoon. Note how long it takes to get a real answer. You will eventually need help with something. Finding out your platform's support quality before a crisis is much better than finding out during one.

What is the cheapest Constant Contact alternative?

Mailchimp has a free tier for up to 500 contacts and 1,000 emails per month. Brevo also offers a free plan with up to 300 emails per day. Which alternative is best for e-commerce?

Klaviyo is purpose-built for e-commerce and connects deeply with Shopify, WooCommerce, and BigCommerce.
