Why Constant Contact Pricing Matters More Than You Think

Most small businesses pick an email marketing tool by going to a pricing page, squinting at a table full of checkboxes, and picking the cheapest option. The problem with that approach is that the cheapest option per month is not always the cheapest option in practice.

Here is what most comparison articles skip: email marketing platforms charge you based on the number of contacts you store, not just the number of emails you send. A platform that looks cheap at 500 contacts can become the most expensive option once you hit 2,500 contacts. And a platform with a great free tier might lock you out of the automation features you actually need, forcing you onto a paid plan anyway.

Constant Contact prices are worth scrutinizing because the platform sits squarely in the mid-market — not the cheapest, not the most expensive, but arguably the best value for a business that wants real deliverability, real support, and real automation without a six-month learning curve.

Consider a real-world example: a bakery in Nashville used Constant Contact to send a single weekly email to 800 customers announcing the weekend specials. Average open rate: 38 percent. Average revenue increase on weekends following an email: $340. Their Lite plan costs them $17 per month. The math is not complicated.

What You Actually Get at Each Constant Contact Price Point

Constant Contact currently offers three main tiers. Here is what each one includes at the base contact level (500 contacts):

Lite — from $12/month

Unlimited email sends, 300+ templates, drag-and-drop editor, basic list segmentation, contact management, and 1 GB of image storage. You also get real-time reporting so you can see open rates and click rates within minutes of sending. Phone and chat support is included — this is not a feature you get on every platform's entry tier.

Standard — from $35/month

Everything in Lite, plus automated welcome series, behavioral email sequences (send a follow-up only to people who did not open the first email), event marketing tools, contact scoring, and the ability to schedule social posts directly from the dashboard. For most established small businesses, Standard is the sweet spot.

Premium — from $80/month

Everything in Standard, plus custom automation paths, advanced segmentation, A/B subject line testing across your full contact list, and priority phone support. If you are running an online store with more than 5,000 contacts or managing email for multiple brands, Premium earns its cost quickly.

Prices rise as your contact count grows. At 2,500 contacts, Lite is $30/month, Standard is $55/month, and Premium is $110/month. At 10,000 contacts, expect to pay roughly $65, $110, and $195 respectively. These numbers are publicly available on Constant Contact's pricing page and are accurate as of July 2026.

How to Get Maximum Value From Constant Contact at Any Price Point

Paying for a tool and using a tool are two different things. Here are six specific actions that directly increase your return on whatever Constant Contact plan you choose.

1. Set Up Your Welcome Email the Day You Sign Up

A welcome email sent within the first hour of someone joining your list gets an average open rate of 50 percent — roughly double what a standard campaign gets. Go to Contacts → Automation → Welcome Email and build a simple two-sentence introduction. Do this before you do anything else. It takes 10 minutes and it starts working for you immediately.

2. Clean Your List Every 90 Days

Constant Contact charges by contact count. Contacts who have not opened any email in six months are not customers — they are a cost. Filter your list by "has not opened in 180 days," send one re-engagement email ("Are you still interested? Click here if you want to stay on our list"), then delete everyone who does not click. This keeps your monthly cost down and your deliverability rate up at the same time.

3. Use the Subject Line A/B Test Feature (Standard and Above)

Send half your list subject line A, half subject line B, wait two hours, then send the winning subject line to everyone who has not opened yet. In practice, a better subject line lifts open rates by 5–15 percentage points. On a list of 2,000 people, that is 100 to 300 additional pairs of eyes on your message from a single change.

4. Connect Your E-Commerce Store

If you run a Shopify or WooCommerce store, connect it to Constant Contact in the integrations panel. Once connected, you can send abandoned cart emails automatically — typically recovering 5–15 percent of abandoned carts. For a store doing $10,000 per month in sales where 70 percent of carts are abandoned, recovering even 5 percent of those means roughly $350 in additional monthly revenue. That is more than enough to cover any Constant Contact plan.

5. Publish a Sign-Up Landing Page and Link to It Everywhere

Constant Contact gives you a hosted sign-up landing page on all plans. Copy that link and put it in your Instagram bio, your email signature, your Google Business profile, and a text in your store window. Every new subscriber you add is another person you can reach for free, forever, without paying for ads. The landing page takes five minutes to build and works 24 hours a day.

6. Pay Annually Once You Know the Tool Works for You

Start month-to-month. After two months, if you are sending campaigns and seeing results, switch to annual billing. Constant Contact offers roughly 15 percent off for annual prepayment. On a $35 per month Standard plan, that is about $63 per year back in your pocket. That covers your coffee budget for a month.

Frequently Asked Questions About Constant Contact Pricing

What is the cheapest Constant Contact plan?

The Lite plan starts at $12 per month for up to 500 contacts. It includes unlimited sends, drag-and-drop email creation, list management, and basic reporting. It is the lowest-cost entry point and is more than enough for most brand-new small businesses just getting their first list off the ground.

Does Constant Contact have a free plan?

No, Constant Contact does not offer a permanent free plan. They do offer a free trial period so you can test the platform before you pay. If you need a free tier long-term, Mailchimp allows up to 500 contacts for free, but its feature set is more limited and its paid plans become expensive quickly as your list grows.

How does Constant Contact pricing compare to Mailchimp?

At the 500-contact level, Constant Contact Lite costs $12 per month versus Mailchimp Essentials at $13 per month. By the time you reach 5,000 contacts, the gap widens: Constant Contact Standard is around $55 per month while Mailchimp Standard hits $75 per month. Constant Contact also includes phone support at every paid tier, which Mailchimp does not.

Is Constant Contact good for e-commerce businesses?

Yes. The Standard and Premium plans include Shopify and WooCommerce integrations, abandoned cart emails, and product block templates. You can pull your product catalog directly into an email without building anything manually. For an online store with even modest traffic, automated abandoned cart recovery alone typically pays for the subscription within the first month.

Can I cancel Constant Contact at any time?

Yes, Constant Contact is a month-to-month subscription with no long-term contract required. You can cancel at any time from your account dashboard or by calling their support line. If you prepay annually, you receive a discount but forfeit the remaining months, so start month-to-month if you want maximum flexibility while testing the platform.
