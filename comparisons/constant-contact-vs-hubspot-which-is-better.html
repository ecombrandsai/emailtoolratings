Why the Constant Contact vs HubSpot Question Matters So Much

Small business owners search "Constant Contact vs HubSpot which is better" more than almost any other email marketing comparison. And for good reason: these two tools look like they solve the same problem from the outside, but they are built for completely different types of business.

HubSpot started as a CRM platform. Email marketing was added on top of an existing sales and pipeline management tool. Constant Contact started as a pure email marketing platform and has spent two-and-a-half decades making that one thing as good as possible.

That origin story matters enormously for small businesses. When you pay for HubSpot, a significant chunk of your money funds CRM features, live chat widgets, landing page builders, sales dashboards, and service hub tools that a typical restaurant owner, freelancer, or local retailer will never touch. When you pay for Constant Contact, almost every dollar goes toward making your emails land in inboxes, look great, and get opened.

Constant Contact vs HubSpot: Feature-by-Feature Comparison

Let's break down what each platform actually gives you at the price levels a small business would realistically pay.

Pricing

Constant Contact's Lite plan starts at $12 per month for up to 500 contacts. The Standard plan (which adds A/B testing, scheduled sends, and more automation) starts at $35 per month. Prices scale with your contact list size but remain predictable.

HubSpot's free tier exists but removes automation, removes A/B testing, limits you to 2,000 email sends per month, and slaps HubSpot branding on everything. HubSpot Marketing Hub Starter costs $20 per month for 1,000 contacts. The Professional tier — which unlocks the automation features HubSpot is known for — costs $890 per month. That is not a typo. For most small businesses, HubSpot Professional is simply unaffordable.

Email Templates

Constant Contact offers over 200 mobile-responsive templates organized by industry — restaurants, nonprofits, retail, real estate, and more. The drag-and-drop editor is genuinely easy. You can build a professional-looking newsletter in under 20 minutes on your first try.

HubSpot's template library is smaller and less polished at the Starter tier. The templates work, but they feel more generic. HubSpot's strength is customization through code — which requires technical skills that most small business owners don't have and shouldn't need.

List Management and Segmentation

Constant Contact lets you segment by contact behavior (opened, clicked, didn't open), by signup source, by custom tags, and by demographics. You can build targeted lists in a few clicks without needing to understand query logic.

HubSpot's segmentation is more powerful at higher tiers, using its CRM data to build very precise lists. But that power only activates when you're feeding HubSpot real CRM data — sales interactions, deal stages, customer lifetime value. If you're a small business that doesn't run a formal CRM pipeline, this extra complexity adds cost and setup time without adding email marketing value.

Automation

Constant Contact's automation covers the things small businesses actually use: welcome emails for new subscribers, birthday and anniversary messages, resend to non-openers (a massive open-rate booster), and basic drip sequences. These features start at the Standard plan for $35 per month.

HubSpot's advanced automation — multi-branch workflows, lead scoring, deal-triggered emails — is genuinely impressive but locked behind the $890/month Professional tier. At the Starter tier, HubSpot's automation is actually more limited than Constant Contact's Standard plan at $35 per month.

Deliverability

Constant Contact maintains a deliverability rate consistently above 97%. They have dedicated teams managing sender reputation, spam filter relationships, and IP warm-up processes. This is their core business. Your emails reach inboxes. Full stop.

HubSpot's deliverability is also solid, but because email is one product line among many for a large tech company, it doesn't get the same focused infrastructure investment.

Customer Support

Constant Contact offers phone support, live chat, and email across all paid tiers — including their cheapest plan. Phone support for email marketing software is increasingly rare and genuinely valuable when you're trying to fix a campaign before a sale ends.

HubSpot's support quality depends heavily on your tier. Starter plan customers get email and chat support but no phone access. Phone support is reserved for Professional and Enterprise customers.

When HubSpot Actually Makes Sense (And When It Doesn't)

Fair is fair. There are specific situations where HubSpot is the smarter choice — but they're narrower than the marketing industry press suggests.

Choose HubSpot if: You have a dedicated sales team that already tracks deals in a CRM. You run B2B lead generation where you need to score prospects, track page visits, and trigger emails based on sales pipeline stage. You have a marketing budget that can absorb $890/month. You need live chat, landing pages, meeting scheduling, and email all tightly integrated.

Stick with Constant Contact if: You are a small business owner, solopreneur, or micro-team. Your goal is sending emails to customers and getting them to buy, visit, or come back. You want to spend 30 minutes per week on email marketing, not 30 hours per month managing a platform. You want phone support when something breaks.

The reality is that over 90% of small businesses searching "Constant Contact vs HubSpot which is better" don't need a CRM-driven marketing platform. They need a fast, reliable, easy-to-use email tool that makes their campaigns look professional and actually reach inboxes. That's Constant Contact.

How to Get the Most Out of Constant Contact Starting Day One

Signing up is the easy part. Here's how to hit the ground running and see ROI within your first 30 days.

1. Import Your Existing Contact List Immediately

Don't start from zero. Export your current customers from your POS system, your website's order history, your Google Contacts, or even a spreadsheet of regulars you've built up manually. Constant Contact accepts CSV files and syncs directly with Gmail, Outlook, Shopify, Salesforce, and QuickBooks. Your first audience is already sitting in tools you use today.

2. Set Up a Welcome Email Before You Do Anything Else

A welcome email goes out automatically to every new subscriber the moment they join your list. Constant Contact's automation makes this a five-minute setup. Businesses that use welcome emails see 33% higher engagement from new subscribers compared to those that don't. Write one welcome email today. It will keep working for you every day with zero ongoing effort.

3. Use the Resend to Non-Openers Feature on Every Campaign

After any campaign, Constant Contact can automatically resend the email to everyone who didn't open it the first time — with a different subject line — after 24 to 48 hours. This single feature typically increases your total campaign reach by 25% to 40% with almost no extra work. Turn it on every time.

4. A/B Test Your Subject Lines

On the Standard plan and above, you can send two versions of your email with different subject lines to a sample of your list. Constant Contact automatically sends the winning subject line to the rest of your audience. Even small improvements in open rate compound into significantly more revenue over a year of campaigns.

5. Check Your Click Map After Every Campaign

Constant Contact shows you a visual heat map of which links in your email got clicked and how often. Use this after every send. If your "Buy Now" button is getting ignored but a text link in the body is getting clicks, you know to make your next CTA a text link. Real data, real adjustments, real improvement.

Frequently Asked Questions: Constant Contact vs HubSpot

Is Constant Contact cheaper than HubSpot?

Yes, significantly. Constant Contact starts at $12 per month for up to 500 contacts. HubSpot's Marketing Hub Starter starts at $20 per month but limits you to 1,000 contacts, and its free tier strips out automation and A/B testing. For most small businesses sending regular newsletters, Constant Contact delivers more usable email features per dollar — especially when HubSpot's full automation suite doesn't unlock until $890 per month.

Does HubSpot have better automation than Constant Contact?

HubSpot's automation is more advanced at higher tiers, but those tiers cost $890 per month or more. Constant Contact's automation — welcome series, resend to non-openers, anniversary emails — covers everything a small business actually needs without paying enterprise prices. For most owners, Constant Contact's automation is exactly the right level of complexity: powerful enough to save real time, simple enough to set up in an afternoon.

Can I switch from HubSpot to Constant Contact easily?

Yes. Export your contacts from HubSpot as a CSV file, then import them into Constant Contact. The process takes about 15 minutes. Constant Contact's onboarding team will walk you through it for free by phone or chat. Your existing contact segments, tags, and custom fields all transfer cleanly through the CSV import wizard. You can be up and running with your full list on the same day you decide to switch.

Which platform has better email deliverability?

Constant Contact consistently posts industry-leading deliverability rates above 97%. HubSpot's deliverability is also strong, but Constant Contact has spent 25+ years focused exclusively on email marketing, which means their infrastructure, IP reputation management, and spam-filter relationships are purpose-built for getting your emails into inboxes. For a small business where every sent email counts, that deliverability edge is worth real money.

Who should choose HubSpot over Constant Contact?

HubSpot makes sense if you need a full CRM, sales pipeline tracking, live chat, and marketing automation all under one roof — and you have a budget of $500 per month or more. If you are a small business owner whose primary goal is sending great marketing emails that drive revenue, Constant Contact gives you everything you need at a fraction of the price. Most small businesses will never use more than 20% of what HubSpot charges them for.
